# The Sightline Audit™ — Constraint Intelligence Questionnaire (v6) **Estimated time:** 25–35 minutes **Purpose:** This assessment is designed to identify the constraint quietly running your company — what it's costing you, who owns it, why it hasn't been solved, and what happens if nothing changes. **The test this questionnaire must pass:** A stranger reading only the responses should be able to answer five questions — *What's the constraint? What's it costing? Who owns it? Why hasn't it been solved? What happens if nothing changes?* If the answers don't get us there, the audit can't either. --- ## Section 1 — Business Snapshot 1. Company name 2. Industry 3. Number of employees 4. Approximate annual revenue *(select range: Under $1M / $1M–$3M / $3M–$5M / $5M–$10M / $10M–$30M / Over $30M)* 5. What was your approximate revenue last year? 6. What was your approximate revenue two years ago? 7. What is your revenue goal for the next 12 months? 8. What does your company do? 9. If you could fix one thing immediately, what would it be? > *Don't overthink this — we'll come back to root cause later. Just name the thing you'd fix first.* --- ## Section 2 — Growth & Revenue 10. Where does most new business currently come from? 11. What slows growth today? 12. Do you know what stage of your sales process prospects, opportunities, or customers stall? 13. Approximately what percentage of revenue depends on one individual? *(Less than 10% / 10-25% / 25-50% / 50-75% / More than 75%)* 14. If revenue doubled tomorrow, what breaks first? 15. What opportunity do you believe you're currently missing? --- ## Section 3 — Customer Signals 16. What do customers complain about most often? 17. Why do customers leave? --- ## Section 4 — Bottleneck Detection 18. What problem has existed for more than 12 months — and what is the consequence of not solving it? 19. What does everyone complain about but nobody owns? 20. What gets escalated to leadership most often? > *This may overlap with what you named above — escalation patterns often reveal a different constraint than general complaints.* --- ## Section 5 — Business Impact 21. What problem is currently costing the business the most money? > *This may be the same problem you named in Section 4 — or a different one entirely. Either answer is useful.* 22. What problem consumes the most leadership attention? 23. If this issue disappeared tomorrow, what would improve first? --- ## Section 6 — Dependency Analysis > *These three questions are intentionally specific — each one surfaces a different type of dependency. Answer each one separately even if your answers feel similar.* 24. If your CEO (or you) disappeared for 30 days, what stops? 25. Who would be hardest to replace? 26. What critical knowledge exists in only one person's head? --- ## Section 7 — Visibility & Decisions 27. What information do you wish you had every Monday morning? 28. What are three decisions leadership repeatedly revisits? 29. What metrics matter most? 30. What metrics are currently difficult to access? 31. Where are decisions made with incomplete information? --- ## Section 8 — Technology & AI 32. What software tools do you currently use? 33. What have you attempted to automate? 34. What failed? 35. What work is still highly manual? 36. Where does duplicate work occur? 37. What information do people regularly ask for that should be easy to access? 38. What is your team's attitude toward AI and automation? --- ## Section 9 — Strategic Priorities & Risk 39. What are your top three priorities for the next 12 months? 40. What would be most damaging if it happened? 41. What are your competitors doing that worries you? 42. If those top three priorities succeeded, what would success look like to you? > *Your answer here will be compared against what the diagnostic finds — this is one of the most important questions in the assessment.* --- ## Section 10 — Change Readiness 43. How quickly are you willing to implement changes? *(Immediately / Within 30 Days / Within 90 Days / Within 6 Months / Unsure)* 44. Have previous improvement initiatives succeeded? Why or why not? --- ## Section 11 — Future State & The Real Diagnosis 45. What would success look like in 90 days? > *Think operationally here — not strategy. What would a normal week look like 90 days from now if things improved?* 46. What are you unwilling to change? 47. Of everything you've described above, which THREE issues create the greatest negative impact? Rank them #1, #2, #3 — and explain why. 48. If you had to bet your own money — what do you believe is the REAL reason this problem exists? > *You've just ranked your biggest issues — now step back. In your gut, what's actually causing them?* 49. Anything else we should know? --- ## How this maps to the final report | Questionnaire section | Feeds into | |---|---| | 1 — Business Snapshot (incl. revenue trend) | Context for everything; revenue trajectory (Q5-7) is often the single most important fact in the entire intake | | 2 — Growth & Revenue | **Revenue Leakage Analysis™** (conversion/capacity leakage); candidate findings for **The Line™** | | 3 — Customer Signals | External validation — constraints customers feel before leadership names them; feeds **The Line™** and **"What You Think vs. What's Actually Happening"** | | 4 — Bottleneck Detection | **Constraint Map™** (Process category); consequence framing (Q18) feeds **Financial Impact** | | 5 — Business Impact | **Revenue Leakage Analysis™** / **Financial Impact** — the dollar figures and "what improves first" framing | | 6 — Dependency Analysis | **Constraint Map™** (People category); **Risk Map**; often the source of **The Line™** itself | | 7 — Visibility & Decisions | **Constraint Map™** (Decision-Making + Data categories) | | 8 — Technology & AI | **AI Opportunity Architecture™** (the centerpiece deliverable); starting-point calibration; non-AI constraints named honestly, referred out where appropriate | | 9 — Strategic Priorities & Risk | **Executive Decisions Required™**; **Risk Map**; Q42 directly sets up the "What You Think vs. What's Actually Happening" comparison | | 10 — Change Readiness | **30/60/90 Day Plan** pacing; Q44 informs how the plan must look DIFFERENT from what's failed before | | 11 — Future State & Real Diagnosis | Q45 → near-term operational framing; Q47 (ranking) → compares leadership's priorities vs. actual constraint vs. financial impact; Q48 (bet-your-own-money) → **"What You Think Is Happening vs. What's Actually Happening"** | --- ## The core design principle for the analysis layer Lumi's example is the standard: a weak analysis produces FOUR findings (slow growth, founder dependency, competition, response time). A strong analysis produces ONE: > *"The business is not constrained by competition. The business is constrained by founder-dependent decision-making, which creates delayed responses, inconsistent execution, and customer frustration. Competition is simply exposing the weakness."* Every section of this questionnaire exists to feed that single sentence — **The Line™** — from a different angle. The analysis layer's job is compression, not enumeration.
