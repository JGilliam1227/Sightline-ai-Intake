Questions

Before you book — the honest answers.

We've never used AI at all. Is this still for us?

Yes — and honestly, that's often the easiest audit. The same diagnostic applies whether you're starting from zero or starting from scattered tools. We build the line from wherever you currently stand.

We already use ChatGPT and a couple of other tools. What does the audit add?

Most businesses in this position have tools but no strategy connecting them to revenue. The audit identifies what's working, what's not, and what's missing — then sequences the next moves so the tools you already have start pulling in the same direction.

How is the final price determined?

During the intake call, we look at your team size, the number of workflows involved, and the complexity of what we're assessing. A 12-person business with straightforward operations sits closer to $2,500. A 45-person business with more moving parts sits closer to $5,000. You'll know the exact number before anything is booked.

Do you implement the plan for us afterward?

No — and that's intentional. The audit is built so your team can execute it without us. If you'd like help with implementation after you've seen the plan, that's a separate conversation — never assumed, never required.

Why 24–72 hours? Doesn't a real audit take longer?

Most of what takes other consultants weeks is meetings, not analysis. Tier 1 delivers in 24 hours — fully automated. Tier 2 adds human review and a live session, delivered in 72. Both compress into days because clarity has a shelf life — the longer it takes to get the plan, the more has already changed by the time you receive it.

What if the audit tells us AI isn't the right move right now?

Then that's exactly what you'll be told. The audit's job is clarity, not a sales pitch for AI adoption. If the highest-value move in your business right now has nothing to do with AI, the plan will say so.
