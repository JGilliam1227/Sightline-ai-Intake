The Line™

Here is what's actually happening.

The Constraint

Meridian is not constrained by sales capacity, market demand, or design quality. Meridian is constrained by an estimation process that was never engineered — it was inherited — and is now the single bottleneck between $4.15M and $5.5M. Every downstream symptom — founder firefighting, lost bids, margin erosion, sales-engineering friction — is this one bottleneck expressing itself in a different room.

01

$600K–$800K in confirmed lost bids annually. Founder-calculated. 15–20 high-value bids lost per year because competitors quote in 48 hours while Meridian takes 5–7 business days.

02

Revenue plateaued and declined with no change in demand. $4.2M → $4.15M. The pipeline fills. The bottleneck lives between discovery and the proposal.

03

The founder is the bypass valve. Sales escalates high-value bids directly to the founder to skip the engineering queue. This is proof the estimation process cannot function at business velocity without founder intervention.

04

The prior fix failed for the right reason. The ERP add-on couldn't parse architectural blueprints. The problem was correctly identified. The wrong tool was purchased.

05

Customers are naming it directly. "Your build quality is exceptional, but it takes far too long to get a final price." Leaving on process speed, not craft.

Why This Matters Now

National competitors are actively entering the Pacific Northwest market and winning legacy accounts through speed-to-quote alone. Meridian's quality advantage means nothing if the proposal never arrives. The competitive window is measured in the next bid cycle.

The 30/60/90 Day Path™

No ambiguity about what comes first.

Critical: The first 30 days produce something tangible before any new technology is introduced. Trust is built before adoption is requested.

30 Days

Foundation — no new tools

→Knowledge-capture sessions with both engineers. Build the Estimation Rules Document. Frame as "building Meridian's IP."
→Standardize the sales-to-engineering handoff with a one-page RFP Intake Form. Immediate reduction in back-and-forth.
→Build the RFP scoring rubric (paper). Triage inbound by margin and fit before escalation. Eliminate low-margin rush jobs.
→Establish baseline metrics: avg turnaround, win/loss ratio, founder interventions/week.

60 Days

Constraint reduction

→Deploy AI blueprint parsing tool using the rules database as the foundation. Pilot on 5–10 archived blueprints. Validate against engineers' manual calculations.
→Build HubSpot → E2 ERP integration via Make.com. Eliminate data re-keying at every handoff.
→Deliver the first 48-hour quote on a real RFP. Document it. Show the team.

90 Days

Scale preparation

→Full AI estimation brief workflow on all standard RFPs. Engineers review and approve — not build from scratch.
→Launch the estimation queue dashboard. Real-time visibility into active blueprints, time-in-queue, trailing turnaround.
→Remove founder from standard pricing approvals. Define escalation threshold. Everything below it flows without intervention.
→Re-audit: Baseline was 5–7 days. Target: consistent sub-48-hour turnaround on standard RFPs.

If nothing changes

The constraint keeps running the company.

Meridian's revenue has already plateaued and declined — $4.2M to $4.15M — despite no reduction in inbound demand. This is the early signature of a capacity ceiling. National competitors are not slowing. Legacy clients absorbing the 5–7 day turnaround out of loyalty are not infinitely patient. The $5.5M target does not become reachable through sales effort when the bottleneck is in estimation.

If this is executed

Same business. Line removed.

A sales rep uploads a blueprint at 9am and receives an AI-generated estimation brief by noon. The engineer spends 60 minutes reviewing — not six hours building. The proposal reaches the contractor that day, while the deal is still hot. The founder's calendar has no pricing escalations. The engineering team — the skeptics — are defending the new workflow because it actually made their jobs better. That's what $5.5M looks like.
